What made you get into Augmented Reality? What inspired you to create your first filter?

I have a bachelor's degree in graphic design and have always been very fascinated with AR filters on Snapchat and Instagram so when I found out that Facebook’s Spark AR platform has moved out of its closed beta and designers are able to publish their own Spark AR effects on the platform I immediately decided to dedicate at least 4-8 hours a day to learning everything I can about the platform and start creating.

So, Kylie Jenner bought the rights to your filter. How did you make that happen?

I am a huge Kylie fan. She’s the main reason I started posting makeup looks on Instagram and I own almost every shade of the Kylie Cosmetics Lip Kits! I was up at 6am on January 7th with my 1 year old daughter and saw Kylie had just posted a story about the Stormi Collection with three beautiful moving butterflies in her hair. I found the look SO beautiful and immediately wanted to recreate that look as a filter. I started right away to draw the butterflies and animate them and then recreate Kylie's beautiful makeup, which was done by Ariel Tejada her makeup artist, to the filter. The filter was ready to publish by 3pm that same day so it took me a little over 8 hours (I skipped breakfast and lunch!). Of course I spent another week perfecting thefilter and the butterfly movement but I wanted to have it ready on the same day and I posted the filter on my feed and left a comment under Kylie's last photo about the filter and also shared with Ariel that his makeup inspired the makeup in my filter. A few days later I got an email from the team and it was one of the best days of my life.

And how much can artists expect to earn through a filter (range)? AR filter creators with a coding background create very technical and complex filters and games. Graphic artists, painters and designers like myself combine our drawing skills with our knowledge of programming and 3D animation to create art that you can wear on your face. Just like any other work the value depends on the complexity and time it takes to complete. For my filters for example I like to start with drawing all themakeup elements in Illustrator and Photoshop and that alone requires many hours. And then I import everything I have created outside of Spark AR and begin the designing and coding process that is needed to complete the project. Filters can take between 1 day to weeks and even months when working with clients so it’s hard to give you a range as it really depends on what we are creating.

Do you also make custom filters for brands or clients?

Yes of course. I’ve worked with celebrities, influencers, businesses and brands.

How do you make these filters - what skills do you need and how do you start?

The more skills the better! Like I wrote above I have a graphic design background and I’m also a painter and makeup artist so I bring together all these skills when I create my filters. Knowledge of coding and script is also a must.

Several researchers are against such filters claiming it causes “Snapchat dysmorphia.” What’s your take on it?

My filters do not include facial warping or deformation. I do not change the shape or size of the users face or any of their facial features in my filters. This change is what causes the dysmorphia you describe and it’s actually banned in Instagram. In Novemver 2019 Instagram stopped approving filters relating to plastic surgery because they have been found to be harmful to mental health. People have become dissatisfied with their natural looks because they want to look like the filters with a smaller nose, bigger eyes and lips. The eyes, nose, lips and everything you see in my filters is your own. I add layers of makeup and shading that is completely achievable in real life with makeup asyou can see in my makeup photos that I post without using my filters. Although I don’t use this type of facial morphing in my filters I personally am not against it at all. I feel people should be free to decide what they would like to do with their face and filters should be fun and not taken seriously. I see nothing wrong with having bigger lips with a filter and in fact you don’t require plastic surgery to get bigger lips so I think Instagram should rethink their ban in the future and allow artists and users to make their own decision and choice.

Since you’re based in Dubai, do you think people’s makeup preferences differ depending on where they’re from?

Women in the gulf region love heavy makeup and have been wearing the full glam face for years before it became so trendy on Instagram. I grew up admiring the beautifully drawn faces and eyes of women around me here. When I used to go home to Canada every summer everyone was more natural and wore a lot less makeup but I see that this is has definitely changednow and women everywhere are experimenting more with makeup.

As a makeup artist, what are a few important tips you can give us to achieve these looks IRL?

Watch my makeup tutorials and stories on Instagram! I use the same style and technique to apply my makeup in real life. I love contouring my cheeks with different shades of bronzer and blush, using eyeliner and strip lashes to achieve dramatic eye looks and contouring my lips with lipliner, different shades of lipstick and adding lipgloss for a more plump look.

What do you think is next in terms of Instagram filters? Where do you see this industry going?

It’s such an exciting time right now because people are discovering filters and enjoying them so much and learning they can have or create their own filters too! This means that soon many Instagram users will have their own filter galleries. It’s definitely going to grow and get more complex by the day. I’m super excited and can’t wait to see where this will take me.

Volv is centered around culture shifters - those who are multifaceted and want to change the status quo. How do you embody that?

I’ve always been the odd artistic one amongst serious number focused or technical individuals and I really enjoy bringing color and art into my interactions. I was the only Graphic Designer getting a Master’s Degree in Business Administration in my class (2004 Sauder School of Business at UBC Canada). And I often felt like my views about the importance of design, branding, color, made people roll their eyes simply because they didn’t understand what I was talking about or thought it was too easy or unimportant. I get this same feeling now with some of my peers in the Spark AR community who come from a coding background and can write very complex codes but don’t know anything about makeup and not too excited byit. But luckily I have found a group of amazing artists who are equally passionate about beauty filters and we enjoy exchanging tips and tricks and together our filters get hundreds of billions of impressions per week.

If you can share one piece of advice with Millennials, what would it be?

Work hard and stay focused on your goals. Nothing is impossible if we want it hard enough.
